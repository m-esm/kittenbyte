How it works

Three swipes, two taps, gigabytes back.

1

Swipe through your library, like you swipe through a dating app.

Left for delete, right for keep. The card tilts and tints as you drag, so you always know which way you're going. No menus, no thumbnails grids, no second-guessing.

2

Photos pile up in a queue. Nothing is gone yet.

Every "delete" swipe adds to a running tally at the top of the screen. You see exactly how much you've queued and how many MB you're about to reclaim. Change your mind anytime.

3

Review the lineup, hit one button, done.

Tap the queue to see every photo before it leaves. Confirm with a single press, and KittenByte hands the list to iOS or Android's native delete flow. Your phone restores them for 30 days if you blink.

Why people stick with it

Designed to be used,
not abandoned.

Cleanup apps usually feel like accounting software. KittenByte feels like a game with rewards, undo, and a cat that gets sad when you swipe wrong.

Undo any swipe

Hit the undo button after a misfire. Decisions are stacked, not committed, until you say so.

Sort by what matters

Oldest first, largest first, video-only, screenshots-only. Hunt down the worst offenders quickly.

Milestone rewards

Every 10 reviews, Byte pops up with a tally and a cheer. Cleanup that pays you back in tiny dopamine hits.

Native delete, recoverable

We hand off to iOS PhotoKit and Android MediaStore. Photos sit in Recently Deleted for 30 days, so nothing is gone forever.

Haptics & sound

Every keep, delete, and milestone gets its own thump and chime. Optional, calibrated, and very satisfying.

One codebase, two phones

Built with Kotlin Multiplatform and Compose. Identical experience on iPhone and Android, no compromises.

Meet Byte

The cat
who watches you decide.

Byte is a Scottish Fold drawn entirely in Compose. He bobs, blinks, tracks your swipes, and gets visibly nervous when you're about to delete a photo of someone he likes. He has no opinions about your love life, only your storage.

No external assets, no Lottie, no GIFs. Byte is 100% vector and animates at 60fps on every device he runs on.
